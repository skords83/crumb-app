# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A small circle of bakers — the owner plus a handful of household members and baking friends, not the general public. They're home bakers focused on sourdough and bread, using Crumb both at the counter mid-bake (phone, hands often floury) and away from the kitchen when planning ahead (browsing recipes, scheduling the next bake). Trust matters: it's a shared personal system, not a public product, so everyone in the circle already knows what the app is for.

## Product Purpose

Crumb is a shared recipe library and bake planner for bread and sourdough. It stores and organizes recipes so they're easy to find again, and it calculates bake timing backward (Backplan) from when the bread needs to be ready, so nobody has to do that math by hand. Success looks like: never guessing when to start a bake, and always finding the right recipe fast, even as the shared library grows.

## Brand Personality

Warm, crafted, precise.

## Anti-references

- The generic cold SaaS dashboard look: navy/blue corporate palettes, flat icon-in-a-box metric cards, gradient hero banners. Crumb is a kitchen tool, not an analytics product.
- The saccharine food-blog aesthetic: pastel doodles, cutesy icons, stock-photo food styling, recipe-blog ad clutter. Crumb should feel like a trusted personal notebook, not a monetized content site.
- Gamified timers or playful stopwatch treatments for bake timing. Backplan is precision infrastructure the baker relies on, not a toy — the tone should feel calm and exact, never novelty.

## Design Principles

1. **Precision without sterility** — Backplan's timing math must feel exact and trustworthy, but the surrounding UI stays warm and tactile, never clinical.
2. **Recipe-first recall** — finding or re-finding a specific recipe should always be the fastest path in the app, even as the shared library grows.
3. **Warmth through material, not decoration** — warmth comes from color, type, and texture choices (crumb/crust cues), not from cute illustration or ornament.
4. **Respect the ritual** — bread baking is slow and hands-on; the interface should never feel rushed, urgent, or gamified.
5. **Personal, not public** — designed for a small trusted circle. No growth-marketing patterns: no upsells, no social proof, no onboarding funnels.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA. No specific known needs beyond that — maintain solid color contrast, full keyboard navigation, and a reduced-motion alternative for any animation.
